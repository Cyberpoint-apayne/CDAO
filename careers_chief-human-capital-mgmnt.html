Strategic Engagement and Policy Directorate

Chief, Human Capital Management, GG-15

KEY ROLE:

The JAIC is looking for a Chief Human Capital Officer that is dedicated to building and sustaining the new artificial intelligence workforce across the Department of Defense, including but not limited to the Military Departments and Services, Office of the Secretary of Defense, and the DoD Components. In this position you will oversee a large team that will establish DoD recruitment, development, and retention programs, policies, and standards that will inform how the Department will manage, train, and equip the workforce that will be developing and delivering AI capabilities tools, technologies, and products. An ideal candidate for this role would have extensive experience in leading the development and implementation of human capital plans, workforce strategies, workforce forecasting, competency assessments, and workforce development, training, and education programs.

KEY RESPONSIBILITIES

  • Manage the development and implementation of the Human Capital Plan.
  • Plan, direct and coordinate a variety of human capital projects and assignments.
  • Manage and lead the human capital plan through the full lifecycle from development to implementation.
  • Garner feedback and ensure human capital program concerns are addressed.
  • Establish accreditation standards for AI curriculums.
  • Formulate and implement project plans, policies, and guidelines for AI training objectives and goals.
  • Develop and manage AI workforce development.
  • Manage and plan technical training objectives by work roles, ensuring all AI course offerings are included.
  • Establish partnerships with other DoD agencies, state agencies, academia, and industry to ensure the JAIC stays current in human capital practices.
  • Represent the JAIC in Government wide meetings and conferences on Human Capital Strategy related topics.
  • Formulate policies and guidance for effective management.
  • Manage and set priority of projects, and establish timeframe for project completion.
  • Evaluate effectiveness of quality standards to meet mission requirements.
  • Present briefings and presentations, provide guidance, and justify decisions to upper management and executives.

KEY QUALIFICATIONS

  • Experience developing and implementing a Department-level or equivalent human capital management program for a new emerging technology mission, including but not limited to the development of innovative workforce programs and policies that facilitated an Enterprise approach for recruitment, development, and retention of technical professionals.
  • Experience leading a robust workforce planning initiative across a large organization that consisted of baseline, future state, gap analysis, and strategy development.
  • Experience in managing a team of 10 or more people.
  • Strong knowledge of human capital practices, principles, laws, and policies.
  • Strong knowledge of human capital techniques, trends, and emerging technology.
  • Prior experience in DoD human capital work.
  • Experience in creating and preparing recommendations for legislation.

CONDITIONS OF EMPLOYMENT

Applicants must be able to obtain and maintain the appropriate security clearance levels for this position. Applicants may be subject to initial and random drug testing for this position, if applicable.

TO APPLY

Please submit a detailed application package (cover letter/statement of interest, resume, and unofficial transcripts) and inquires to the JAIC Hiring Box at: osd.JAIC-Hiring@mail.mil
