Plans and Analysis Directorate

Cyber Program Protection & OPSEC
Analyst GG-0854/1550/14

INTRODUCTION

The Joint Artificial Intelligence Center (JAIC) was established to enable the Military Departments and Services, Joint Staff, Combatant Commands (CCMDs), Office of the Secretary of Defense (OSD), and other DoD Components, to serve on a Cross-Functional Team (CFT) to swiftly introduce new capabilities and effectively experiment with new operational concepts in support of DoD's warfighting missions and business functions with the overarching goal of accelerating the delivery of AI-enabled capabilities, scaling the Department-wide impact of AI, and synchronizing DoD AI activities to expand Joint Force advantages. A CFT is an inter-disciplinary team including military officers, acquisition officials, and technical experts.

POSITION SUMMARY

The Program Protection/OPSEC Analyst is the Subject Matter Expert (P2/OPSEC SME) providing support to DoD artificial intelligence/machine learning (AI/ML). Analyzes Critical Information (CI) to assess developments, trends, and threat implications. Applies comprehensive knowledge of cyber/CI to develop estimates and projections. Provides advice on CI operations and planning capabilities and requirements. Presents and defends positions effectively within the operational and planning communities. Leads team(s) on special projects or studies of considerable scope and depth critical to the resolution of CI issues and problems. Interprets complex legislation, regulatory, and policy guidance to prepare recommendations for management. Ensures Critical Program Information (CPI) developed by the program is properly protected. Leads in the identification of CPI and in the identification of Critical Information and develops countermeasures (CMs) to protect both. Ensures horizontal protection is established and maintained, in compliance with DoD requirements. Builds/tailors CI Awareness and Reporting training packages and produces CI Threat Assessments.

POSITION REQUIREMENTS

Expert skill in planning, organizing, and directing studies or projects, and justifying, negotiating or settling matters involving significant or controversial issues related to Insider Threat Program operations and capabilities.

In-depth knowledge of policies, regulations, directives, procedures and precedents applicable to Operations Security Planning and Strategy, and the ability to develop regulatory guidance and policy for OPSEC programs.

Comprehensive understanding of DoD Protection functions such as Anti-Terrorism, High-Risk Personnel, Personnel Security, Cybersecurity, Physical Security, Law Enforcement, Installation Access Control, Intelligence and Counterintelligence and how they interrelate and support the DoD Insider Threat Program in the deterrence, detection, and mitigation of insider threats.

Expert knowledge of quantitative and qualitative techniques for analyzing and measuring effectiveness and efficiency of exercise planning and execution. Must have skill in adapting those techniques to measure and improve effectiveness of CI operations.

Mastery of security specialties in resolving major conflicts in policy and program objectives. Specific knowledge and understanding of applicable public laws, executive orders, and DoD and DoD policies and regulations.

Mastery level knowledge of identification and protection of CPI, horizontal protection methodologies; development, application, execution of Security Classification Guides, and Special Access Program Security.

CONDITIONS OF EMPLOYMENT

If education is a requirement for the position, applicants must provide a copy of their unofficial transcript(s) from their degree awarding institution(s), along with a resume.

Applicants must be able to obtain and maintain the appropriate security clearance levels for this position. Applicants may be subject to initial and random drug testing for this position, if applicable.

ADDITONAL INFORMATION

This is a Cyber Excepted Service (CES) Position: Work Category (Professional); Work Level (Senior/3)

TO APPLY

Please submit a detailed application package (cover letter/statement of interest, resume, and unofficial transcripts) and inquires to the JAIC Hiring Box at: osd.JAIC-Hiring@mail.mil
