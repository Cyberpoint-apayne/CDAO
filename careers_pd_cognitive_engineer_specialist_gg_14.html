Products Directorate

Cognitive Engineer , GG-14

INTRODUCTION

The Joint Artificial Intelligence Center (JAIC) was established to enable the Military Departments and Services, Joint Staff, Combatant Commands (CCMDs), Office of the Secretary of Defense (OSD), and other DoD Components, to serve on a Cross-Functional Team (CFT) to swiftly introduce new capabilities and effectively experiment with new operational concepts in support of DoD's warfighting missions and business functions with the overarching goal of accelerating the delivery of AI-enabled capabilities, scaling the Department-wide impact of AI, and synchronizing DoD AI activities to expand Joint Force advantages. A CFT is an inter-disciplinary team including military officers, acquisition officials, and technical experts.

POSITION SUMMARY

The JAIC is looking for a Cognitive Engineer to join our Infrastructure Division. In this role, you will be responsible for the deployment of Infrastructure and other programs utilizing best practices, methods, processes and procedures. You will also ensure efficient handling of changes and maintain the proper balance between the need for rapid incremental change and the potential detrimental impact of changes.

KEY RESPONSIBILITIES

  • Establish and continuously improve a configuration management function.
  • Continuously monitor the state of multi-cloud infrastructure environment and maintain its operational security and stability.
  • Identify vulnerabilities and shortcomings in software, hardware, cloud, configuration, and processes to ensure known risks associated with planned changes.
  • Prepare configuration documents and maintain the quality, integrity, and security of the configuration management database.
  • Schedule audits and assists in implementing audit recommendations.
  • Apply best practices and configuration management tools to automate changes to the configuration of the infrastructure.
  • Ensure all changes to the infrastructure are documented and carried out.
  • Analyze and revise program to keep pace with Industry best practices or when functional enhancements occur.
  • Develop reporting requirements and specifications on continuous process improvements while working with JAIC Cybersecurity to implement new reports.
  • Organize and participate on advisory boards.
  • Develop and apply principles theories and techniques of computer science, software engineering, hardware design, enterprise computing, commercial cloud adoption, DevSecOps, and information engineering.
  • Identify, develop and infuse new methodologies and technologies into JAIC architecture.
  • Develop training material and documentation to reflect program enhancements.
  • Performs other related duties as assigned.

POSITION REQUIREMENTS

  • Must be a US Citizen with ability to possess and maintain a security clearance.
  • Expert knowledge of concepts, theories, and practices and techniques of computer science and/or computer engineering.
  • Expert knowledge of network systems and applications, systems documentation, configuration management, system integration, security architecture, and testing.
  • Knowledge of a wide range of analytical techniques such as mathematical modeling, and systems analysis.
  • Knowledge of network security, vulnerability assets, security assessment tools and security engineering.
  • Proven knowledge of the functional process and technical alternatives sufficient to ensure inoperability, integration, and interface requirements.
  • Expert knowledge in state of the art practices such as Agile, DevSecOps, Infrastructure as Code, and Configuration as Code.
