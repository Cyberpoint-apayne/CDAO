Capability Delivery Directorate

Configuration Manager
GG-0854/1550/14

INTRODUCTION

The Joint Artificial Intelligence Center (JAIC) was established to enable the Military Departments and Services, Joint Staff, Combatant Commands (CCMDs), Office of the Secretary of Defense (OSD), and other DoD Components, to serve on a Cross-Functional Team (CFT) to swiftly introduce new capabilities and effectively experiment with new operational concepts in support of DoD's warfighting missions and business functions with the overarching goal of accelerating the delivery of AI-enabled capabilities, scaling the Department-wide impact of AI, and synchronizing DoD AI activities to expand Joint Force advantages. A CFT is an inter-disciplinary team including military officers, acquisition officials, and technical experts.

POSITION SUMMARY

Serves as the Configuration Manager, which supports the development of JCF Infrastructure Configuration and Change standardized methods, processes and procedures to facilitate efficient and prompt handling of all changes and maintain the proper balance between the need for change and the potential detrimental impact of changes. Identifies vulnerabilities and shortcomings in software, hardware, configuration, and processes, to ensure known risks associated with planned changes are communicated and mitigated prior to implementation. Analyzes software and hardware vulnerabilities, develops mitigating strategies to protect sensitive information and systems; applies state of the art technologies to develop engineering solutions to security problems or unprecedented threats; provides technical advice to managers on cyber incidents; coordinates responses to cyber incidents of national significance; directs and manages resolutions for addressing cyber-security issues; and creates operational policies and procedures based on new security technologies or threats.

POSITION REQUIREMENTS

A bachelor’s degree in engineering (0854) or computer science (1550). (*See Education Requirements Fact Sheet)

Expert professional knowledge of the concepts, theories, practices and techniques of Computer Science or Computer Engineering. This knowledge is applicable to a wide range of duties to the extent that the incumbent is capable of applying new developments and experienced judgement to solve novel or obscure problems. The incumbent must possess skills sufficient to extend and modify existing techniques and develop new approaches for use in solving a variety of computer/systems engineering problems.

Requires expert professional knowledge combined with knowledge of network systems and applications. Also requires knowledge of systems documentation, configuration management, standards, system integration, security architecture, cost analysis, data administration, systems integration, and testing.

Knowledge of the functional processes and technical alternatives sufficient to ensure that interoperability, integration, and interface requirements are met and conflicts resolved.

Knowledge of a wide range of analytical techniques such as mathematical modeling, systems analysis, statistical analysis econometrics, etc.

Knowledge of Information Assurance policies, procedures, network security, vulnerability assessments, security assessment tools, and security engineering. Also requires knowledge of the mission, objectives, terminology, and management practices of the activity, to recognize probable areas of interaction and overlap between proposed applications and existing systems.

CONDITIONS OF EMPLOYMENT

If education is a requirement for the position, applicants must provide a copy of their unofficial transcript(s) from their degree awarding institution(s), along with a resume.

Applicants must be able to obtain and maintain the appropriate security clearance levels for this position. Applicants may be subject to initial and random drug testing for this position, if applicable.

Positions which perform information security as an inherent, primary purpose of their position: Information Assurance Certification is a condition of employment. This position includes information assurance (IA) work as a paramount duty requirement. Per DoD 8570.01-M, the incumbent of this position must achieve the appropriate IA certification within six months of assignment of these duties. A waiver of this six month requirement may be granted per DoD 8570.01-M, C3.2.4.2 or C3.2.4.3. Failure to receive the proper IA certification may result in removal from this position.

ADDITONAL INFORMATION

This is a Cyber Excepted Service (CES) Position: Work Category (Professional); Work Level (Senior/3)

TO APPLY

Please submit a detailed application package (cover letter/statement of interest, resume, and unofficial transcripts) and inquires to the JAIC Hiring Box at: osd.JAIC-Hiring@mail.mil
