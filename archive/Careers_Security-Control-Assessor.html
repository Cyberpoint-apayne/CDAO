Capability Delivery Directorate

Security Control Assessor
GG-2210-14

INTRODUCTION

The Joint Artificial Intelligence Center (JAIC) was established to enable the Military Departments and Services, Joint Staff, Combatant Commands (CCMDs), Office of the Secretary of Defense (OSD), and other DoD Components, to serve on a Cross-Functional Team (CFT) to swiftly introduce new capabilities and effectively experiment with new operational concepts in support of DoD's warfighting missions and business functions with the overarching goal of accelerating the delivery of AI-enabled capabilities, scaling the Department-wide impact of AI, and synchronizing DoD AI activities to expand Joint Force advantages. A CFT is an inter-disciplinary team including military officers, acquisition officials, and technical experts.

POSITION SUMMARY

Serves as Security Control Assessor, ensures the implementation and compliance of JCF Infrastructure Cybersecurity program within the JAIC. Provides training and support to organizations and POC’s in order to meet compliance of CYBERCOM Directives. Monitors and coordinates JAIC’s Cybersecurity program compliance RMF reporting, analyzing, validating and adjudication of A&A evidence, artifacts and POA&Ms. Oversees the Cybersecurity readiness posture within JAIC. Supports the formulation, interpretation, dissemination, and enforcement of Cybersecurity policy and procedures. Performs staff assignments related to Cybersecurity program planning, monitoring, and/or execution in consultation with DoD CIO, NMI Cybersecurity or technical staff members. Ensures confidentiality, integrity, availability and non-repudiation of the JCF Infrastructure, and that assets and data are maintained through the planning, analysis, development, implementation, maintenance, and enhancement of its information systems, cybersecurity programs, policies, procedures, and tools. Ensures JCF Infrastructure and its’ IS products are in compliance and align with the JAIC’s baseline security posture and appropriately address any issues arising from a non-compliant state. Ensures leadership appoints persons in RMF roles in writing and provides oversight to ensure that they follow established DoD, JFHQ DoDIN, USCYBERCOM, and JAIC guidance, policies and procedures. Oversees the A&A process for all JAIC information systems, PIT, enclave, and networks, to include review and endorsement of the Security Authorization Packages (SAP).

POSITION REQUIREMENTS

Security+ or Higher Certification.

Comprehensive and expert knowledge of RMF and cybersecurity laws, policies, regulations, directives guidance, practices, procedures and professional standards, to provide authoritative advice and guidance pertinent to the JAIC Cybersecurity Program during all stages of the system security management lifecycle, to include requirements development, application development, maintenance, data management and network analysis.

Mastery of, and skill in applying, advanced information systems and cybersecurity laws, Federal and DoD regulations, Executive directives, Federal information systems protocols, systems security certification and accreditation requirements and processes, and core information systems security concepts, principles and requirements to an enterprise protection environment.

Expert knowledge and skill in applying interrelationships of multiple integrated information systems networks and specialties, the JAIC’s IT architecture, and information systems/cybersecurity concepts, principles and practices, to manage the implementation, monitoring and compliance of security programs designed to anticipate, assess, and minimize system vulnerabilities.

Expert knowledge of technology infrastructures using Firewalls, VPN, Data Loss Prevention, IDS/IPS, Web-Proxy and Security Audits.

Expert knowledge of a vast range of information systems vulnerabilities and protection concepts, principles and practices to resolve extremely difficult and complex security and information systems controls challenges.

Comprehensive knowledge of a wide range of work management and project techniques and methods for the assessment and improvement of complex information systems/cybersecurity audit controls for the JAIC architecture. Ability to perform professional analysis and formulate authoritative recommendations regarding enterprise information systems/cybersecurity compliance requirements.

Requires advanced analytical thinking, detailed understanding of cybersecurity methodologies, meticulous attention to detail, outstanding problem-solving skills, and ability to work comfortably under pressure and deliver on tight deadlines.

Ability to lead a team of cybersecurity professionals to deliver adaptive security architecture capabilities; and provide security expertise as part of an established functional capability team.

CONDITIONS OF EMPLOYMENT

Security+ or Higher Certification.

If education is a requirement for the position, applicants must provide a copy of their unofficial transcript(s) from their degree awarding institution(s), along with a resume.

Applicants must be able to obtain and maintain the appropriate security clearance levels for this position. Applicants may be subject to initial and random drug testing for this position, if applicable.

ADDITONAL INFORMATION

This is a Cyber Excepted Service (CES) Position: Work Category (Professional); Work Level (Senior/3)
