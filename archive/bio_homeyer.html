BIOGRAPHY

Dr. Stephen T. Homeyer

Deputy Director

Dr. Stephen Homeyer is currently on a Joint Duty Assignment from National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency (NGA), serving as the Deputy Director of Department of Defense's Joint Artificial Intelligence Center (JAIC). In this role, he is involved in all aspects of the stand-up of this new organization.

Dr. Homeyer previously served as the Deputy Director of NGA's Research Directorate, where he led the effort to transform the organization's structure, business practices, policies, and culture. Prior to this assignment, Dr. Homeyer served as Senior Strategy Advisor for Science and Technology in the Office of the Director of National Intelligence. During this time, he designed and led the first joint ODNI/NGA S&T Strategic Study entitled "Strategic Opportunities for GEOINT" with participation of government experts from across the IC.

From 2004-2011, Dr. Homeyer served as the Director of NGA's Basic and Applied Research Office, where he expanded the Office's expert scientific staff capability (from 40 personnel in 2005 to 160 in 2010) by hiring the best and brightest Ph.D., M.S., and B.S. level graduates in mathematics, physics, engineering, and computer science. Because of his persistent focus on teaming scientists closely with their operational counterparts, NGA advanced numerous new technologies into in-theater demonstrations, and subsequently, operational use during his tenure.

Prior to joining the NGA, Dr. Homeyer was the Chief of the Federal Bureau of Investigation's (FBI) Counterterrorism and Forensic Science Research Unit from 1999 to 2004.

Dr. Homeyer holds a Ph.D. in Chemistry and M.S. in Inorganic Chemistry from Northwestern University. He graduated Cum Laude with Honors in Chemistry from Huntingdon College in Montgomery, Alabama with a dual B.S. degree in Chemistry and Mathematics. He also earned his A.A. and A.S. degrees from Oakton Community College in Des Plaines, Illinois. He has received over 16 major technical and managerial awards during his professional career. He holds seven patents and has published 15 scientific papers in a variety of peer-reviewed technical journals. Dr. Homeyer has also been an adjunct faculty member at several colleges throughout his career, teaching classes at the undergraduate and graduate levels.
