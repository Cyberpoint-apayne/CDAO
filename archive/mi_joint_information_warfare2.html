Joint Information Warfare

Driving Information Advantage Through AI

Joint Information Warfare (JIW) Mission Initiative team seeks to apply AI and ML solutions to ensure information, space, and electromagnetic spectrum advantage in defense of the United States, Allies, Partners, and its Interests.

With our mission partners, JIW is applying AI-enabled capabilities to:

  • Cyberspace Operations: Operational integration of AI/ML powered solutions to defend, attack, manage and exploit in the cyberspace operational domain
  • Electromagnetic Spectrum Operations (EMSO): Operational integration of AI/ML powered solutions to defend, attack, manage and exploit across EMSO
  • Space Operations: Integration of AI/ML solutions to deter conflict, defeat aggression, and deliver space combat power to the Joint Force
  • Information Operations: Operational integration of AI/ML powered solutions to exploit adversarial information, in addition to defending our nation’s pubic and protected information

MADHAT is a software search tool, developed at the University of Maryland’s Laboratory of Physical Sciences, and operationalized by the JIW team. It uses AI to automate and accelerate network behavior analysis of IP addresses in a computer network. Cyber analysts in the High-Performance Computing Modernization Program (HPCMP), responsible for the Defense Research and Engineering Network (DREN), wanted to improve their ability to detect malicious cyber activity on their network. They worked with JIW to employ MADHAT. It was configured and trained, and discovered suspicious activity, in seconds compared to the weeks it may take human analysts. MADHAT helps HPCMP’s Cybersecurity Service Provider protect the DREN's data, computer systems, and approximately 4,300 users, at nearly 200 government laboratories, test universities, and industrial locations.
