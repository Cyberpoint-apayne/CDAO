AI and Data Accelerator (ADA)

The AI and Data Accelerator (ADA) transforms how Combatant Commands conduct globally-integrated data management, including both warfighting and business decision analytics, and provides a data foundation to enable workflow and C2 automation capabilities.

ADA pulls together resources, advanced solutions from across DoD, and cross-functional expertise to build and implement scalable, responsive and integrated capability [data?] sets for the eventual adoption of AI solutions.

ADA is:

  • Integrated: Led by JAIC & the DoD Chief Data Officer (CDO), with partners including Joint Staff, DDS, CIO, USD(R&E), USD(I&S), and others. This effort integrates each CCMD with people, platforms, and partners across the JADC2 community.
  • Innovative: ADA will accelerate work that is already planned and funded. ADA will facilitate the development of global, open architecture data C2 solutions that enable modern data management, advanced analytics, and automated business and warfighting workflows.
  • Invested: This effort has the attention of our most senior DoD leaders. DSD Hicks personally announced this effort and the Vice Chairman, JROC, and JADC2 CFT have endorsed the requirement. This work supports implementation of the DoD Data Strategy, JADC2 Strategy, and DSD Data Decrees.
  • Iterative: ADA is a methodology to rapidly identify and scale solutions through a series of implementation sprints and exercises, each one purposely building understanding through successive and incremental learning. This three-year pilot will help identify, prioritize, and sustainably address Joint warfighting challenges and set future requirements.
  • Impactful: ADA is focused on rapidly delivering decision advantage from the battlespace to the boardroom.

ADA teams will work closely with each Combatant Command, tailoring data, and building algorithm and infrastructure solutions. ADA will provide embedded elements to enable operators and developers to rapidly iterate and employ agile methodologies ultimately building a foundation of scalable solutions throughout other Combatant Commands and the Department.

 

Responsible AI

Advancements in artificial intelligence (AI) are transforming national security, the global economy, and society at large. Adopting AI will preserve and extend America’s military advantage in a digitally competitive world. As the United States Department of Defense (DoD) embraces AI, it is imperative that this technology is integrated in a manner consistent with our national values, shared democratic ideals, and commitment to lawful and ethical behavior.

The DoD AI Ethical Principles outlines appropriate safeguards for a technology that continues to evolve. The Principles build on and complement the existing ethical, legal, safety, and policy frameworks that are the hallmark of the Department. To take these high level Principles and instantiate them into DoD practices, the Department is adopting Responsible AI (RAI).

Within the DoD, RAI is an approach to the design, development, deployment, and use of AI systems to advance their trustworthiness and ensure their ethical employment. RAI is manifested through testing standards, accountability checks, employment guidance, human systems integration, and safety considerations.

Initial RAI efforts that have been implemented within the DoD’s Joint Artificial Intelligence Center include:

  • Leveraging industry best practices to develop and test tools, such as AI Data Cards and Model Cards, that consider relevant risks from the outset of an AI project and reduce the unintended consequences.
  • Integrating RAI into the Tradewind acquisition business model by piloting AI ethics assessments and checkpoints with vendors.
  • Including contract clauses to demonstrate how a capability addresses the DoD AI Ethical Principles and aids in mitigating ethical risks.
  • Including RAI instruction in the 2020 DoD AI Education Strategy.
  • Promoting RAI internationally with like-minded defense forces through the AI Partnership for Defense [link to International section]

The DoD is taking a holistic, integrated, and disciplined approach to RAI through an RAI Strategy and Implementation Plan. The enterprise-wide efforts will help to scale RAI in a manner that embraces innovation, while adopting a risk management approach to AI development. Through RAI, the DoD provides our Service members, the American public, and our partners and allies justified confidence in DoD’s AI-enabled systems.

"Responsible AI is the place where cutting-edge tech meets timeless values. The Department’s use of AI must reinforce our democratic values, protect our rights, ensure our safety, and defend our privacy,”
- Secretary of Defense, Lloyd Austin


DoD Ethical Principles for AI

  • Responsible: DoD personnel will exercise appropriate levels of judgment and care, while remaining responsible for the development, deployment, and use of AI capabilities.
  • Equitable: The Department will take deliberate steps to minimize unintended bias in AI capabilities.
  • Traceable: The Department’s AI capabilities will be developed and deployed such that relevant personnel possess an appropriate understanding of the technology, development processes, and operational methods applicable to AI capabilities, including with transparent and auditable methodologies, data sources, and design procedure and documentation.
  • Reliable: The Department’s AI capabilities will have explicit, well-defined uses, and the safety, security, and effectiveness of such capabilities will be subject to testing and assurance within those defined uses across their entire life cycles.
  • Governable: The Department will design and engineer AI capabilities to fulfill their intended functions while possessing the ability to detect and avoid unintended consequences, and the ability to disengage or deactivate deployed systems that demonstrate unintended behavior.

Mission Initiatives

Using an interdisciplinary and cross-functional approach, the JAIC partners with Services, Combatant Commands, the Joint Staff, Combat Support Agencies, and program offices to accelerate the adoption and integration of Artificial Intelligence across the DoD’s warfighting communities and support enterprises. We currently support five Mission Initiatives:

Health and Business Processes has two primary focuses:

  • Business Process Transformation applies scalable AI and automation to areas such as business, acquisition, law, finance, policy, HR, and training to improve efficiency, accuracy, and overall operations.
  • Warfighter Health integrates AI to improve warfighter medical readiness, human performance, and combat medicine.

Joint C2, Maneuver and Fires (JC2MF) integrates AI-enabled applications into systems, processes, and platforms to gain decision speed, agility, and strategic advantages over our adversaries.

Joint Information Warfare (JIW) provides AI-enabled information solutions that contributes to information advantage in all domains and the electromagnetic spectrum through assurance, resilience, flexibility, and security. Link to: Joint Information Warfare

Joint Logistics (JL) optimizes warfighter readiness, helps sustain operations, and delivers cost savings by integrating AI capabilities in the supply chain, distribution, and maintenance services.

Joint Force Protection (JFP) uses AI to transform the joint mission areas aligned against unpredictable threats – such as Humanitarian Assistance & Disaster Relief, Countering Weapons of Mass Destruction, and Force Protection.

 

Testing & Evaluation

A critical aspect of Responsible AI implementation is technology testing and evaluation. A robust test and evaluation or T&E process is critical to ensure that military systems support the Warfighter in accomplishing their mission.

The DoD has decades of experience putting new technologies through a gauntlet of testing – from full range of early developmental to operational tests - to ensure they deliver critical performance and capability. New technologies demand new testing and evaluation techniques to address potential new weaknesses and failures. AI and machine learning (ML) is no exception.

ML and AI performance in the operational environment is directly tied to training data quantity, quality, and representativeness. Training data must adequately represent the full diversity and complexity of the operational environment, and test data covering a sufficient breadth of scenarios and number of examples is necessary to provide operators and decision makers insight into model performance.

The JAIC test and evaluation team, comprised of experts in test design, AI technology, military operations, systems integration, and human factors, rigorously and objectively test our AI capabilities for operational effectiveness, robustness, resiliency and alignment with the DoD's ethical principles for artificial intelligence.

Ensuring DoD AI systems earn and deserve the trust of the warfighters that count on them.

The JAIC is enabling the DoD by:

  • Leading a Department-wide community of experts to develop new T&E approaches and update DoD T&E standards based on AI system requirements.
  • Publishing T&E playbooks and best practice guides that are freely available to any government organization or partner.
  • Providing shared access to our test infrastructure and cloud enabled capabilities, including our cloud native AI test harness.

Enabling our DoD partners AI success:

  • Augmenting the DoD’s ability to quickly develop and implement AI, ML, and natural language processing (NLP) capabilities that improve safety evaluations of all systems.
  • Launched a first-of-its-kind T&E bulk purchase agreement that allows any DoD service or agency to quickly access leading AI T&E vendors with a pre-negotiated best value contract.

Accelerating the adoption of AI requires our warfighters trust their AI-enabled capabilities when the mission is on the line.

Strategy & Policy

Strategy and Policy’s focus is to solve the policy and cultural problems that have prevented DOD from continuously building, fielding, and scaling game-changing AI technologies that deter and win wars.

The JAIC provides Department-wide leadership to address critical needs and develop and provide AI policy that enables acceleration of integrated AI delivery. Working with our DoD partners, policy and guidance includes the DoD Artificial Intelligence Strategy, the National Defense Strategy, DoD AI Governance, and Responsible AI implementation.

Education and Training

An AI-ready workforce is key to an AI-ready DoD. To ensure the Department can fully take advantage of the power that AI will bring, it is pivotal that our workforce understands, adopts and embraces an AI-enabled future. This requires a both AI technical experts and non-AI experts who are fluent in the aspects of AI that are increasingly relevant for their work.

The JAIC is leading the DoD’s workforce transformation to upskill current personnel and develop a long-term AI talent pipeline for DoD and beyond. As part of this, the JAIC is:

  • Developing and disseminating training that improves the skill sets of the current workforce and encourages cross collaboration across the commands through the interaction of its students learning from a common curriculum.
  • Collaborating across partners to identify vital requirements to attract, develop, track, and retain a diverse, proficient, responsible, and mission-ready AI workforce across the Department.
  • Reforming workforce policies to ensure military and civilians with AI-relevant skills are hired, aligned, and retained to ensure bring their expertise to bear. [Link to DoD AI Education Strategy]
  • Leading the DoD AI Workforce Subcommittee, comprised of stakeholders from across the Department to collaborate and synchronize efforts and share best practices that drive positive reforms.

Acquisition – Rapid Done Right

Enabling the DoD to rapidly acquire innovative AI solutions that solve current and future challenges – from back office to the battlefront, The JAIC offers DoD an expanding suite of innovative, decentralized procurement vehicles that allow for rapid AI delivery and purchasing of key AI services and enabling tools. From AI Delivery to T&E, to AI Talent, and demo testing, the JAIC’s acquisition team is here to help connect our DoD partners with leading edge AI capabilities found in industry and academia.

Each vehicle is deliberately designed to address a specific AI-related challenge the DoD faces and enables the Department to deliver AI capability to the Warfighter and maintain a “whole-of-nation” approach to keeping U.S. competitive advantage over its adversaries.
