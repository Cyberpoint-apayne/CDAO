Light-Based AI Chips

As both the quantity and quality of AI chips demanded by the public and private AI sectors grows, vendors are looking for new breakthrough technologies that can dramatically increase the capacity of today’s AI chips. To do so, some are re-assessing the fundamentals of how semiconductors operate.

One startup, Lightmatter, is building AI chips that use light, as opposed to traditional transistors, to perform key operations related to executing AI algorithms. The company claims that making the switch enables it to execute state-of-the-art AI models, for instance the BERT language model, five times faster than existing top-of-the-line chips. Staying on the cutting edge of AI may require having access to emerging forms of High Performance Computing.

Robotic Dogs Enter the Commercial Labor Force

The NYPD is deploying a dog-shaped robot constructed by Boston Dynamics for policing in a system that it has dubbed “Digidog”. The platform has been used specifically to scout areas for police officers, ensuring, for instance, that an apartment is empty and/or that everyone inside is unarmed before the police execute a warrant.

The product, Spot, began selling last June, and has largely been deployed in industrial settings where operations are required that are unsafe for human personnel. The robot has seen deployment for hazardous material inspection, and during the COVID-19 pandemic the platform has even been used to communicate with patients in hospital triage sites.

Link: Digidog, a Robotic Dog Used by the Police | The New York Times  

Protein Folding Problem Solved by AI

You may have heard that researchers at Google Deepmind were able to use a deep-learning system, which they have titled “Alphafold”, to create a new state-of-the-art for predicting the shape which proteins, the building blocks of biology, will fold.

Alphafold uses a combination of existing deep learning methods to predict the physical shape that proteins will fold into given the sequences of amino acids that make them up. Some biology researchers spend large sections of their career trying to solve this problem for individual proteins, so the system is likely a harbinger of continued improvements in biotechnology from advances in artificial intelligence.
