Capability Delivery Directorate

Architect, GG-0854/1550-14

INTRODUCTION

The Joint Artificial Intelligence Center (JAIC) was established to enable the Military Departments and Services, Joint Staff, Combatant Commands (CCMDs), Office of the Secretary of Defense (OSD), and other DoD Components, to serve on a Cross-Functional Team (CFT) to swiftly introduce new capabilities and effectively experiment with new operational concepts in support of DoD's warfighting missions and business functions with the overarching goal of accelerating the delivery of AI-enabled capabilities, scaling the Department-wide impact of AI, and synchronizing DoD AI activities to expand Joint Force advantages. A CFT is an inter-disciplinary team including military officers, acquisition officials, and technical experts.

POSITION SUMMARY

This position serves as an Architect in the JAIC Infrastructure and Platforms Division. The incumbent designs, develops, and implements an infrastructure strategy for the JAIC. Applies the principles, theories, and techniques of computer science, software engineering, hardware design, and information engineering to research, develop, identify, and infuse new methodologies and technologies into JAIC architecture and design. Advises the Division Chief on architecture engineering and technical issues associated with the various mission initiatives across the JAIC. Develops and formulates solutions to technical problems, and identifies and applies proven or emerging technologies and methodologies that advance, enhance, and improve the JAIC’s infrastructure strategy. Develops advanced concepts and directs innovative research on information systems and related areas to advance the accomplishment of JAIC strategic mission objectives. Leads the development of comprehensive data analytics, algorithms, and modeling for both functional and technical processes. Uses automated statistical tools in developing and employing metrics to stabilize, manage, and enhance services. Leverages technical expertise, to conduct research in computability, computational complexity, and the analysis of algorithms to enhance the ability of DoD to provide information services in a direct and tailored fashion.

POSITION REQUIREMENTS

  • A bachelor’s degree in engineering (0854) or computer science (1550). (*See Education Requirements Fact Sheet)
  • Mastery of architectural and/or engineering theories, concepts, and practices, as well as the practices and principles of one or more computer engineering disciplines to develop JAIC’s enterprise-wide approaches and implementation for infrastructure, data management, and software application development.
  • Extensive knowledge of software/hardware/network/security/data management integration approaches for federated sharing of the operating system’s run time environment, architectural components of infrastructure services and support applications, as well as mission applications.
  • Authoritative knowledge of the architecture and operation of state-of-the-art software intensive Information Technology systems and knowledge in analysis, design, implementation, operation, test, evaluation, and maintenance of software systems and computer systems architecture, computer networking, information assurance, database administration, system administration, and software development tools and techniques.
  • Broad and comprehensive knowledge in the technical aspects of planning, design, development, test, certification, delivery, operation, and sustainment of complex automated information systems and services.
  • Professional and expert knowledge and understanding of Federal and DoD information management concepts, methods, and practices; information and data engineering; life-cycle management policies and procedures; interoperability systems testing; and, cost and resource analysis.
  • Skill in oral and written communication sufficient to maintain liaison and correspondence with engineers in other organizations who are expert consultants on processes, trends and innovations; and to advise and consult with upper management.

CONDITIONS OF EMPLOYMENT

  • If education is a requirement for the position, applicants must provide a copy of their unofficial transcript(s) from their degree awarding institution(s), along with a resume.
  • Applicants must be able to obtain and maintain the appropriate security clearance levels for this position. Applicants may be subject to initial and random drug testing for this position, if applicable.
  • Positions which perform information security as an inherent, primary purpose of their position: Information Assurance Certification is a condition of employment. This position includes information assurance (IA) work as a paramount duty requirement. Per DoD 8570.01-M, the incumbent of this position must achieve the appropriate IA certification within six months of assignment of these duties. A waiver of this six month requirement may be granted per DoD 8570.01-M, C3.2.4.2 or C3.2.4.3. Failure to receive the proper IA certification may result in removal from this position.
  • A bachelor’s degree in computer science (1550) or engineering (0854) or Mathematical Statistics (1529) or Operational Research (1515)(*See Education Requirements Fact Sheet)

ADDITONAL INFORMATION

This is a Cyber Excepted Service (CES) Position: Work Category (Professional); Work Level (Senior/3)
