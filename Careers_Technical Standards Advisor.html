Strategic Engagements and Policy Directorate

Technical Standards Advisor
GG-0854/1550-14

INTRODUCTION

The Joint Artificial Intelligence Center (JAIC) was established to enable the Military Departments and Services, Joint Staff, Combatant Commands (CCMDs), Office of the Secretary of Defense (OSD), and other DoD Components, to serve on a Cross-Functional Team (CFT) to swiftly introduce new capabilities and effectively experiment with new operational concepts in support of DoD's warfighting missions and business functions with the overarching goal of accelerating the delivery of AI-enabled capabilities, scaling the Department-wide impact of AI, and synchronizing DoD AI activities to expand Joint Force advantages. A CFT is an inter-disciplinary team including military officers, acquisition officials, and technical experts.

POSITION SUMMARY

The incumbent supports the Strategic Engagement and Policy Directorate and serves as Technical Standards Advisor responsible for guiding the JAIC and DoD in the development and implementation of standards for AI scaling across the DoD enterprise, U.S. interagency partners, and foreign allies. Develops and publishes policies governing technical standards in the areas of data management, AI safety, security, and assurance, test and evaluation and verification and validation of algorithms. Formulates long-range policies and develops and implements strategies and management guidelines related to technical standards. Leads periodic program reviews to develop an overall assessment of program progress and success that reflects the consolidated opinion of multiple experts. Develops recommendations for technical policy issuances pertaining to best practices for AI deployment. Participates in current and long-range planning meetings to ensure that goals and objectives are fully and effectively related to the overall JAIC and DoD strategy. Identifies aspects of current scientific and industry practices that fail to meet the complex test and evaluation challenges of national security programs and work to improve those practices.

POSITION REQUIREMENTS

A bachelor’s degree in computer science (1550) or engineering (0854)(*See Education Requirements Fact Sheet)

Mastery level knowledge of scientific research practices and statistical analysis methods, critical thinking, and analytical skills to develop technical standards policy for the DoD AI programs.

Mastery of, and skill in applying advanced theories of computer science, in order to provide technical direction to programs related to Department of Defense Artificial Intelligence goals and objectives. Comprehensive knowledge of advanced theories, concepts and principles of computer science sufficient to keep abreast of research and development by reviewing pertinent reports and other related data to ensure incorporation of state-of-the art methods into the technical standards development process.

Exceptional skill in effectively and tactfully communicating complex information, both orally and in writing, in order to provide clear, comprehensive advice and guidance, present detailed reports and findings, and render suitable recommendations to promote improvements in efficiency and effectiveness.

Extensive knowledge and experience developing and publishing policies to govern technical standards in the areas of data management, AI safety, security, and assurance, test and evaluation and verification and validation of algorithms.

Comprehensive knowledge and understanding of the theories, concepts, and practices related to systems and software development lifecycle activities and their requirements. Mastery level knowledge of major DoD processes related to capability/requirements definitions, information system planning, developmental testing, operational testing, programming, budgeting, acquisition, and management.

Mastery knowledge of the applicable Federal laws, regulations and circulars related to information systems development lifecycle activities. Mastery knowledge of process improvement and change management planning, strategies, techniques, analysis, implementation, and performance measures.

Exceptional understanding of advanced software strategies, processes, and methodologies for Information Technology systems to include software systems acquisition, integration, interoperability and testing, and resource/budget accountability. Involves major decisions and actions, which have a direct and substantial effect on meeting JAIC and DoD commitments.

Extensive managerial and leadership skills and imagination combined with the seasoned administrative skills required to organize and function in a climate of continuously shifting guidelines. Utilizes an unusually high degree creativity and critical judgment in the role of a software engineering expert, as well as in the application and use of advanced information technology techniques.

CONDITIONS OF EMPLOYMENT

If education is a requirement for the position, applicants must provide a copy of their unofficial transcript(s) from their degree awarding institution(s), along with a resume.

Applicants must be able to obtain and maintain the appropriate security clearance levels for this position. Applicants may be subject to initial and random drug testing for this position, if applicable.

Positions which perform information security as an inherent, primary purpose of their position: Information Assurance Certification is a condition of employment. This position includes information assurance (IA) work as a paramount duty requirement. Per DoD 8570.01-M, the incumbent of this position must achieve the appropriate IA certification within six months of assignment of these duties. A waiver of this six month requirement may be granted per DoD 8570.01-M, C3.2.4.2 or C3.2.4.3. Failure to receive the proper IA certification may result in removal from this position.

ADDITONAL INFORMATION

This is a Cyber Excepted Service (CES) Position: Work Category (Professional); Work Level (Expert/3)
