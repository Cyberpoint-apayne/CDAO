The health of DoD’s military service members is critical to U.S. national security, as it impacts the readiness and effectiveness of the fighting force. The DoD spends nearly $50B annually on the Military Health System. The JAIC’s Warfighter Health team focuses on improving medical outcomes using military health data to provide successful AI applications.

With our partners, we are focused on:

Warfighter Readiness that includes:

  • Medical imaging such as radiology, pathology, and chest x-ray
  • Medical Evaluation Readiness Information (MERIT) matches service members’ personnel and medical records against known disability and treatment timelines. MERIT also delivers AI-enhanced business practices and clinical decision support to enable doctors to detect conditions leading to degraded readiness.

Human Performance includes a suicide intervention and prevention risk model, the Commander's Risk Mitigation Dashboard, that will improve a leader’s situational awareness of when their team is at risk for a variety of destructive behaviors and allow them to make more informed decisions about an individual’s readiness and resilience. Warfighter Health In Action

The JAIC, in partnership with DIU and Google, is fielding Augmented Reality Microscopes (ARMs) to ten military treatment facilities (MTFs) and VA hospitals CONUS and OCONUS. These microscopes will leverage computer vision and machine learning to identify cancerous regions on pathology tissue slides. The ARM will initially be able to identify four types of cancer (breast cancer, pancreatic cancer, mitotic cell divisions, and cervical dysplasia) with the opportunity for the development of additional models. The final product will allow the pathology community to scan and digitize, process, and classify pathology slides on-demand.
