JAIC History

January 2019: Lt Gen Jack Shanahan became the first Director of the Joint AI Center
February 2019:

  • White House Executive Order on Maintaining American Leadership in Artificial Intelligence

September 2020:
  • JAIC hosts the first DoD AI Symposium and Exposition that brings together the DoD AI ecosystem.
  • JAIC facilitates first-ever International AI Dialogue for Defense

  • JAIC hosts second DoD AI Symposium and launches Tech Exchange to highlight innovation in industry and academia
  • AI and data Accelerator initiative announced by the Deputy Secretary of Defense

Who We Are

The Joint Artificial Intelligence Center team is comprised of the very best from across government, industry and academia. Each JAICster brings their unique expertise to bear on the future of AI in the Department of Defense. Across the DoD, our people and partners are driving exciting change at the intersection of AI, large scale data systems, and some of the most important missions of our time.

Team JAIC is mission-oriented, undeterred in focus, and driven to ensure our warfighters have the best tools to help safeguard American citizens, support our allies and partners, and improve the affordability and effectiveness of military operations.

About the JAIC

The United States Department of Defense (DoD) established the JAIC in 2018 to seize upon the transformative potential of Artificial Intelligence technology for the benefit of America's national security. At the time, AI activity in the DoD was limited to early-stage research being conducted in organizations like DARPA and the military research labs. Only project MAVEN, an early-stage pathfinder for applying AI to DoD intelligence activities was actively working with commercially mature AI systems in operational environments.

Since its establishment, the DoD and the JAIC have broadened the scope of its pathfinding in broader integrated warfighting, while ensuring the benefits of AI-enabled capabilities are expanded to areas such as Department business operations, military logistics, defense support to civil authorities in disaster response, cyber, and health.

As the Department progressed along its AI journey, the JAIC has matured to serve the Department’s dynamic needs. In February 2021, “JAIC 2.0” shifted the focus from developing specific “proof of concept” AI capabilities to primarily providing the Department enablement capabilities such as enterprise technical architectures, technical services, advisory services, policy reforms, AI governance, and workforce reforms that will lead the DoD through foundational cultural change necessary for the transition from hardware- to software-oriented processes.

Some of the key foundational elements that the JAIC brings to the Department are:

  • Deployment and maintenance of the Joint Common Foundation that brings modern DevSecOps practices to DoD
  • Inculcation of holistic, integrated, and disciplined approach to Responsible AI [Link to page] that focuses, not only on the technology, but on necessary organizational operating structures and culture to ensure confidence that DoD AI-enabled systems will be safe and reliable and will adhere to ethical standards
  • Development of mission [link to page]-focused AI- and AI-enabling capabilities that support activities across the DoD enterprise, from the battle front to the back office
  • Delivery of the AI and Data Accelerator (ADA) , a series of rapid, agile implementation experiments that provide Combatant Commanders the required data and AI-enabled automation tools to achieve decision advantage
  • Management of rapid AI acquisition vehicles that work at the speed of relevance

With our partners across the DoD’s AI ecosystem, Team JAIC is persistently and passionately driving toward an AI-enabled tomorrow.

Five pillars of the DoD AI Strategy

In alignment with the DoD AI Strategy [Link to DoD AI Strategy], the JAIC recognizes and anticipates the implications of new technologies on the battlefield, rigorously defines the military problems anticipated in future conflict, and fosters a culture of experimentation and calculated risk-taking by:

  • Delivering AI-enabled capabilities that address key missions
  • Scaling AI’s impact across DoD through a common foundation that enables decentralized development and experimentation.
  • Cultivating a leading AI workforce
  • Engaging with commercial, academic, and international allies and partners
  • Leading in military ethics and AI safety
