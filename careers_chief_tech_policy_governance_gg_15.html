Strategy and Policy Directorate

Chief, Technical Policy and Governance Division , GG-15

INTRODUCTION

The Joint Artificial Intelligence Center (JAIC) was established to enable the Military Departments and Services, Joint Staff, Combatant Commands (CCMDs), Office of the Secretary of Defense (OSD), and other DoD Components, to serve on a Cross-Functional Team (CFT) to swiftly introduce new capabilities and effectively experiment with new operational concepts in support of DoD's warfighting missions and business functions with the overarching goal of accelerating the delivery of AI-enabled capabilities, scaling the Department-wide impact of AI, and synchronizing DoD AI activities to expand Joint Force advantages. A CFT is an inter-disciplinary team including military officers, acquisition officials, and technical experts.

POSITION SUMMARY

  • Location: Arlington, VA | USA
  • Job Type: Permanent
  • Salary: $148,484k-176,300k
  • Open & Close Dates: 04/22/2022-05/05/2022
  • Number of Openings: 01

The JAIC is looking for a Professional to join the Strategic Engagements and Policy Division as Policy Chief, reporting to the Director of the JAIC. In this role you will guide and inform DOD strategic processes, establish joint training programs, support interagency and intergovernmental requirements, and build/sustain international partnerships.

DUTIES & RESPONSIBILITIES

  • Establish and manage AI governance through building and leading communities of interest, organizing DoD-wide AI and synchronizing DoD AI activities.
  • Draft and coordinate DoD issuances, Secretary of Defense guidance memoranda and providing input to other strategic documents, such as Defense Planning Guidance, on AI technical policy, ethics, standards, and other issues as appropriate.
  • Oversee DoD and JAIC participation in AI technical standards working groups across the DoD, US interagency, and the international community.
  • Develop and publish policies governing technical standards for AI in the areas of data management, AI safety, security, assurance, test and evaluation, and verification and validation of algorithms.
  • Build AI training program to support Defense-wide AI adoption and scaling.
  • Influence key strategic documents in the Department's planning, programming, budgeting, and execution processes.
  • Coordinate with other DoD Components in developing and implementing novel strategic and operational concepts for AI across the Joint Force.
  • Develop and coordinate DoD positions in support of interagency policy meetings on matters pertaining to Al, including White House-led meetings.
  • Plan and assigns work to be accomplished based on priorities, consideration of the difficulty and requirements of assignments, and employees' capabilities, including setting/adjusting short-term priorities and schedules for completion of work.
  • Performs other duties as assigned.

KEY REQUIREMENTS

  • Expert knowledge of subject matter expertise of Technical Policy.
  • Experience in building AI training programs.
  • Expert knowledge of subject matter expertise in AI technical standards.
  • Expert knowledge of governance and policy.
  • Ability to build strong relationships with a broad community that defines and promotes best practice standards.
  • Ability to maintain awareness and understanding of best practice standards in the deployment of artificial intelligence ethical principles and frameworks across industry, non-governmental organizations, U.S. government entities, and international organizations.
