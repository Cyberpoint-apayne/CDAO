Missions Directorate

Chief, Missions Directorate, GG-15

INTRODUCTION

The Joint Artificial Intelligence Center (JAIC) was established to enable the Military Departments and Services, Joint Staff, Combatant Commands (CCMDs), Office of the Secretary of Defense (OSD), and other DoD Components, to serve on a Cross-Functional Team (CFT) to swiftly introduce new capabilities and effectively experiment with new operational concepts in support of DoD's warfighting missions and business functions with the overarching goal of accelerating the delivery of AI-enabled capabilities, scaling the Department-wide impact of AI, and synchronizing DoD AI activities to expand Joint Force advantages. A CFT is an inter-disciplinary team including military officers, acquisition officials, and technical experts.

POSITION SUMMARY

The JAIC is looking for a highly experienced professional to serve as the Chief Of Missions in the Missions Directorate. In this positon, you will report to the JAIC Deputy and Director on matters related to selecting, designing, and executing AI-focused initiatives and projects for operational users internal and external to the Department, direct and manage multiple cross-cutting DoD and National AI mission initiatives to reduce critical mission challenges, including project scoping, implementation, and operational assessment.

KEY RESPONSIBILITIES

  • Lead the selection, design, development, and execution of all National, DoD Enterprise and Component AI mission initiatives and projects for delivering AI solutions across a suite of disciplines and mission areas.
  • Develop, communicate, and maintain an AI mission initiatives roadmap that represents a robust framework for the near-term and long-term development and implementation of DoD and National AI mission initiatives and projects.
  • Provide technical advisement and consultation to Senior leaders on the design, development, and execution of a robust DoD governance structure to facilitate the development and promulgation of all AI Doctrine, Policy, and Transition Plans to ensure the adoption of a new technological capability across multiple organizations internal and external to the Department.
  • Serve as the JAIC’s senior representative and subject matter expert for all AI-related Mission Initiatives with the Joint Staff, DoD, Military Departments, International Partners, Industry, and Academia.
  • Coordinate regularly with industry, academia, and research and development leaders in government, as authorized and appropriate, to identify, analyze, and further near-term AI mission initiatives and projects.
  • Advise the JAIC Director on recruiting leading AI/technology talent into the DoD civilian, uniformed, and contractor workforce.
  • Provide the JAIC Director with recommended courses of action (COA) with respect to all corresponding, AI-focused hiring actions, personnel realignments, etc. affecting product delivery.
  • Represent the JAIC and the Defense Department in technical matters relating to artificial intelligence and/or machine learning policy, planning, and objectives and provides professional and technical engineering expertise in artificial intelligence engineering technology functional areas across Industry, Government, and Academia.
  • Serve as the JAIC’s senior representative and subject matter expert for all AI-related communications with the Joint Staff, DoD, Military Departments, International Partners, Industry, and academia.
  • Represent the JAIC as a Senior Advisor on National Mission Initiatives (NMI) and Component Mission Initiatives (CMI), and other matters of national and defense AI policy.
  • Represent the Department as a Senior Advisor on matters of national and defense AI policy, including on technology, product, or other matters in his/her portfolio such as ethics and safety.
  • In support of the Director, lead on related AI boards and steering groups, and serve on various senior general officer and senior executive-level boards and committees.
  • Develop and communicate the JAIC’s AI Mission Initiatives vision for the DoD Enterprise and the Nation.
  • Establish clear goals for each DoD and National Mission Initiative Team; and define success criteria and key metrics for assessing results achieved.
  • Develop, communicate, and maintain an AI mission initiatives roadmap that represents a robust framework for the near-term and long-term development and implementation of DoD and National AI mission initiatives and projects.
  • Lead the design, development, and execution of a robust DoD governance structure to facilitate the development and promulgation of all AI Doctrine, Policy, and Transition Plans.
  • Provide technical advisement and consultation to Senior leaders on the development of an integrated master schedule to coordinate lines of effort across the Mission Initiative Teams; track progress and identify critical path risks for cross-team execution.
  • Ensure that systems engineering, technical engineering, and program management best practices and lessons learned are being observed.
  • Facilitate communications and integration across the Mission Initiative Teams and the other product delivery functional areas.
  • Performs other duties as may be assigned.

KEY REQUIREMENTS

  • Proven experience in standing-up new structures for technical large scale mission initiatives that includes internal and external stakeholders.
  • Strong public speaking skills.
  • Possess sound judgment, initiative, the ability to work independently and as part of a highly focused team.
  • Military/government background a plus
  • Strong and proven oral and written communications skills
  • Team player and motivator who is comfortable working in an unstructured environment in a rapidly growing DOD start-up
  • Demonstrated leadership and management of teams of from 10 to 200 diverse personnel spread across the country
  • Self-starter who understands the balance between speed and quality
  • Commercial industry experience in developing Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning solutions for the Federal government; a state or local government; or a large corporation.
  • Extensive knowledge of artificial intelligence, machine learning, and data science concepts, methodology, techniques, and technologies.
  • Proven experience working on complex artificial intelligence and machine learning projects, as the Lead Artificial Intelligence/Machine Learning Scientist or Engineer.
  • Experience defining and developing a new artificial intelligence and machine learning capabilities, technologies, and tools.
  • Extensive knowledge of management concepts, principles, methods and practices.
  • Extensive knowledge in full range of product management and engineering software development activities starting from Concept and Exploration, and continues through development, production, and completion of initial fielding of the system/equipment items, and transition to another program office for sustainment and operations as specifically related to artificial intelligence and machine learning enabled solutions.
  • An exceptionally strong understanding of relevant Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning developments in industry, academia, government, with our allies and partners, and with our adversaries; Ability to apply this understanding to inform DoD artificial intelligence mission roadmaps and key decisions.

For more detailed information on the above criteria, please review the OPM Qualification Standards.
