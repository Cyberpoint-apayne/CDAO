Mission Initiatives

Joint Logistics (JL)

Enhancing logistics through AI enabled capabilities offer large dividends as DoD’s spending on logistical support to warfighters averages $150B annually, and up to 70% of platform cost is tied to the lifecycle sustainment of a piece of equipment such as a tank, ship, or aircraft.

Joint Logistics optimizes warfighter readiness, helps sustain operations, and delivers cost savings by integrating AI capabilities in the supply chain, distribution, and maintenance services. Leveraging the DoD’s vast repository of available logistics and maintenance data to provide decision support tools at all levels will enhance the supply chain efficiency, reduce sustainment cost, and increase availability.

With our mission partners, Joint Logistics is applying AI-enabled capabilities to:

  • Predictive Logistics: Develops and applies predictive analytics capabilities across the Joint Logistics Enterprise to optimize readiness, streamline asset deployments, improve contingency planning, and aid commanders' decision making.
  • Predictive Maintenance: Leverages vehicle operational and maintenance data to create AI models that predict or forecast future maintenance needs – reducing downtime and lowering cost of unexpected maintenance.
  • Logistics Data Maturation: Ensures effectiveness of AI models through collection, curation, and cross-examination to improve data quality.

Joint Logistics In Action

Predictive Maintenance for MH-60M Blackhawk Engines: JAIC JL, in partnership with Carnegie Mellon University, and SOCOM‘s 160th Special Operations Aviation Regiment (SOAR), developed an AI-based Engine Health Model (EHM) for MH-60M Blackhawks and their YT706 engines. This model provides critical fleet health information to maintainers and mission planners. Utilizing historical vehicle sensor data, received from an onboard Voice and Data Recorder, the model predicts the probability of an engine fail-to-start event over a 5-25-flight hour horizon.

The U.S. Air Force's HH-60G's Integrated Vehicle Health Management System (IVHMS) 4G accelerometer was designed to capture data used for regime recognition and maintenance planning calculations. However, approximately 8% of the accelerometer measurements are corrupted. Working with Georgia Tech Research Institute, an AI/ML tool, Virtual Sensors/Missing Data (VSTAR), was developed, using a Deep Neural Network (DNN) to reconstruct missing 4G accelerometer data for HH-60G Pave Hawk rotorcraft in order to provide more accurate assessments of vehicle fatigue. More accurate demand forecasting allows supply chains to more accurately predict demand for replacement parts. Additionally, the neural network that powers VSTAR has the ability to work on any sensor network, so the program can be tailored for specific DoD user needs.
