Mission Initiatives

Joint Logistics (JL)

Enhancing logistics through AI enabled capabilities offer large dividends as DoD’s spending on logistical support to warfighters averages $150B annually, and up to 70% of platform cost is tied to the lifecycle sustainment of a piece of equipment such as a tank, ship, or aircraft.

Joint Logistics optimizes warfighter readiness, helps sustain operations, and delivers cost savings by integrating AI capabilities in the supply chain, distribution, and maintenance services. Leveraging the DoD’s vast repository of available logistics and maintenance data to provide decision support tools at all levels will enhance the supply chain efficiency, reduce sustainment cost, and increase availability.

With our mission partners, Joint Logistics is applying AI-enabled capabilities to:

  • Predictive Logistics: Develops and applies predictive analytics capabilities across the Joint Logistics Enterprise to optimize readiness, streamline asset deployments, improve contingency planning, and aid commanders' decision making.
  • Predictive Maintenance: Leverages vehicle operational and maintenance data to create AI models that predict or forecast future maintenance needs – reducing downtime and lowering cost of unexpected maintenance.
  • Logistics Data Maturation: Ensures effectiveness of AI models through collection, curation, and cross-examination to improve data quality.

Joint Logistics In Action

Predictive Maintenance for MH-60M Blackhawk Engines: JAIC JL, in partnership with Carnegie Mellon University, and SOCOM‘s 160th Special Operations Aviation Regiment (SOAR), developed an AI-based Engine Health Model (EHM) for MH-60M Blackhawks and their YT706 engines. This model provides critical fleet health information to maintainers and mission planners. Utilizing historical vehicle sensor data, received from an onboard Voice and Data Recorder, the model predicts the probability of an engine fail-to-start event over a 5-25-flight hour horizon. The 160th SOAR is currently testing the EHM in their Model at the Edge (MaTE) Kit in an operational environment.

Eliminating pre-existing siloes or barriers to AI: The JAIC partnered with the Joint Staff J4 to develop and mature a Joint Logistics Enterprise (JLEnt) group of key logistics personnel/organizations dedicated to the advancement of DoD capability and decision-making through AI. Establishing new lines of communication, this group promotes DoD-wide understanding of AI efforts across the JLEnt, sharing of lessons learned/best practices, and collaboration on AI efforts. This JAIC-coordinated group is becoming a nexus for AI professionals, innovators, and champions to work best approaches to DoD logistics problems. This forum has proven useful to conducting data discovery and brokering connections to solve real-time problems, and developing an AI pipeline that takes use cases from data ingestion through AI capability deployment.
