Capability Delivery Directorate

Operations Research Analyst (Test & Assessment)
GG-1515/1550/1529-14

INTRODUCTION

The Joint Artificial Intelligence Center (JAIC) was established to enable the Military Departments and Services, Joint Staff, Combatant Commands (CCMDs), Office of the Secretary of Defense (OSD), and other DoD Components, to serve on a Cross-Functional Team (CFT) to swiftly introduce new capabilities and effectively experiment with new operational concepts in support of DoD's warfighting missions and business functions with the overarching goal of accelerating the delivery of AI-enabled capabilities, scaling the Department-wide impact of AI, and synchronizing DoD AI activities to expand Joint Force advantages. A CFT is an inter-disciplinary team including military officers, acquisition officials, and technical experts.

POSITION SUMMARY

This position serves as an Operational Research Analyst performing test and evaluation functions supporting National Mission Initiative (NMI) Cross-Functional Teams in the JAIC Capability Delivery Directorate. Formulates and participates in test design activities for the verification and validation of AI/ML systems and solutions in conjunction with the CFTs. Assists the JAIC Lead Operational Research Analyst in the creation of Test and Evaluation Master Plans (TEMP) for JAIC funded efforts. Identifies the technical risks in testing of projects, particularly for assessing the limitations of planned tests on complex AI systems. Coordinates the requirements and issues during the test and evaluation, experimentation and prototyping efforts. Performs engineering/scientific analyses of test data and assists in resolving discrepancies. Monitors technical and user test activities for CFT to keep leadership informed of system progress and challenges. Manages, reviews and makes recommendations of user evaluation and equipment performance reports (EPR) used for subsequent proposals for design changes. Ensures that Engineering Change Proposals (ECPS) and other or materiel developer trade-off proposals meet user requirements. Participates in technical exchange meetings (TEM) with industry and academic institutions to assess the operational relevance and technical quality of T&E technologies. Writes reports and generates briefings to communicate the technical aspects of test plans and assessments to leaders across the JAIC and the Department of Defense.

POSITION REQUIREMENTS

A bachelor’s degree in engineering (0854) or computer science (1550). (*See Education Requirements Fact Sheet)

Mastery of advanced principles and practices of computer engineering or computer science, and communications that enables the employee to serve as an expert in the development and application of specialized test and evaluation techniques, to set up basic testing philosophies, and to develop new concepts for testing and evaluating the operational effectiveness and suitability of assigned systems and/or software solutions.

Mastery of one or more specialized areas of operations research, statistics, computer science and engineering sufficient to apply new analytical developments, methodologies, and findings to studies and analyses of an unprecedented nature.

Extensive knowledge and mastery of research techniques and concurrent ability to obtain information concerning subject studies to perform sound analyses.

Advanced professional knowledge of analytical, mathematical, statistical theories, principles, concepts, methods, and techniques to provide expert judgments concerning the validity of assumptions made and the criteria to evaluate alternatives.

Skill in coordinating extensive projects in assigned areas of responsibility. Projects are characterized by high visibility, unusual urgency, or program criticality.

Skill in identifying actual or potential problem areas, trends, and/or similar factors to improve Joint Artificial Intelligence Center program operations and management systems.

Ability to identify the significant variables and determine their underlying relationships.

Ability to design, build, and use very complex, sophisticated models to simulate real-world environments of many variables.

CONDITIONS OF EMPLOYMENT

If education is a requirement for the position, applicants must provide a copy of their unofficial transcript(s) from their degree awarding institution(s), along with a resume.

Applicants must be able to obtain and maintain the appropriate security clearance levels for this position. Applicants may be subject to initial and random drug testing for this position, if applicable.

ADDITONAL INFORMATION

This is a Cyber Excepted Service (CES) Position: Work Category (Professional); Work Level (Senior/3)

TO APPLY

Please submit a detailed application package (cover letter/statement of interest, resume, and unofficial transcripts) and inquires to the JAIC Hiring Box at: osd.JAIC-Hiring@mail.mil
