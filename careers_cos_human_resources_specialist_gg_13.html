Chief of Staff Directorate

Human Resources Specialist, GG-13

INTRODUCTION

The Joint Artificial Intelligence Center (JAIC) was established to enable the Military Departments and Services, Joint Staff, Combatant Commands (CCMDs), Office of the Secretary of Defense (OSD), and other DoD Components, to serve on a Cross-Functional Team (CFT) to swiftly introduce new capabilities and effectively experiment with new operational concepts in support of DoD's warfighting missions and business functions with the overarching goal of accelerating the delivery of AI-enabled capabilities, scaling the Department-wide impact of AI, and synchronizing DoD AI activities to expand Joint Force advantages. A CFT is an inter-disciplinary team including military officers, acquisition officials, and technical experts.

POSITION SUMMARY

The purpose of this position is to serve as a Human Resources Specialist providing management advisory services for a wide range of human resources management (HRM) areas and other special programs to support the JAIC’s senior leadership.

MAJOR DUTIES

  • Provides strategic and technical guidance, as well as seasoned consultative management advisory services throughout the JAIC on the effective use of HRM to support goals and objectives and produce results that accomplish JAIC’s mission. Staff level advice is provided in a wide range of HRM areas such as, merit promotion, recruitment and placement, management and employee relations, performance management, position management and classification, compensation, civilian and military training and development, military personnel management, workforce planning, reporting, civilian and military recognition and awards, and other special programs. Develops appropriate interventions for management on significant human resources (HR) issues and solutions to especially complex problems of program improvement. Collaborates with DISA and DFAS human resources staff members in the resolution of a variety of complex classification, staffing and employee benefit related issues. Develops and recommends process improvements, corrective actions, and solutions to improve operational efficiencies, streamline processes, and optimize service delivery.
  • Performs program evaluation of assigned HR programs and develops, coordinates and executes improvement/enhancement plans and/or policies. Reviews current and proposed Office of Personnel Management (OPM), and DoD civilian and military directives, current policies and precedents for their applicability to HR functions in the JAIC. Reviews JAIC operations to assure compliance with these directives and ensures legal and regulatory requirements are met. Conducts policy reviews to evaluate the effectiveness of current policies, practices and procedures and ensures consistency in their application across the JAIC organizations. Ensures that JAIC policies and programs comply with statutes and OSD regulations and guidance. Recommends modifications when needed. Distinguishes regulatory issues within the larger context of HR initiatives. Applies key aspects of OPM and DoD Strategic Human Capital Management plans in program oversight and evaluation. Advises based on potential problem areas and necessary actions, alternatives, etc. Identifies areas where program changes need to be made to meet mission requirements. Conducts extensive analyses of available facts, which may be only partially relevant, and applicable directives, laws, etc., in the resolution of problems and adverse trends. Develops procedures and techniques for dealing with especially complex problems in assigned program areas. Serves as a consultant and technical advisor in those especially difficult and complex cases involving issues not previously encountered and particularly controversial matters are at stake.
  • Participates in business process management efforts to include, process improvement, managing metrics, and data analytics to optimize HR efficiency and effectiveness. Assesses performance criteria to ensure key programmatic elements are effectively measured.
  • Monitors measurement metrics to evaluate the health of HR programs. Recommends changes as necessary. Examines strategies, processes, and resources for improving organizational performance or business process efforts, taking into consideration the potential contribution of new and emerging technologies.
  • Compiles personnel and training reports to keep management and JAIC leadership abreast of personnel related matters.
  • Serves as staff advisor and consultant on a diverse spectrum within the JAIC Human Resources Office, conducting day-to-day execution of the JAIC’s civilian/military personnel and manpower management. Provides advisory services on recruitment and placement actions requiring identification and analysis of management staffing problems, and development of solutions considering the total personnel management viewpoint. Provides technical advice to JAIC components in the resolution of staffing problems for all levels and complexities of organizations, to include reorganizations, recruitment difficulties, and technically complex staffing determinations. Prepares personnel action packages for review and subsequent processing. Responds to applicant inquiries and application utilizing JAIC touchpoints to communicate with prospective applicants. Organizes applicant tracker for JAIC hiring box in support of JAIC hiring strategy and initiatives. Develops, establishes, and promulgates JAIC personnel supplemental guidance relating to the Cyber Excepted Service personnel system.
  • Serves as technical advisor in major functions of employee relations (e.g., organizational development, adverse actions, performance management.) Provides policy guidance and advice to JAIC components, day-to-day assistance, interpretation, information and guidance on employee relations problems and issues, to include organizational goal performance, individual performance, performance appraisal systems, pay-for-performance compensation systems, employee relations (ER) training programs, etc.
  • Provides advisory and consultative services to all management levels within the JAIC on specific issues that impact the classification process and effective position management.
  • Provides staff advice and guidance on performance management and pay and compensation as it relates to recruitment and compensation strategies based on regulatory requirements.
  • Assists in facilitating the operational planning, monitoring, and evaluation activities for assigned programs, projects and studies covering all aspects of human resources and manpower management for the JAIC.
  • Prepares and delivers training on assigned programs including the most complex or emerging laws, regulations or issues. Develops and delivers oral and written presentations to senior officials and various audiences.
  • Performs other related duties as assigned.
