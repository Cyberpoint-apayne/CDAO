Chief of Staff Directorate

Facilities Manager, GG-13

INTRODUCTION

The Joint Artificial Intelligence Center (JAIC) was established to enable the Military Departments and Services, Joint Staff, Combatant Commands (CCMDs), Office of the Secretary of Defense (OSD), and other DoD Components, to serve on a Cross-Functional Team (CFT) to swiftly introduce new capabilities and effectively experiment with new operational concepts in support of DoD's warfighting missions and business functions with the overarching goal of accelerating the delivery of AI-enabled capabilities, scaling the Department-wide impact of AI, and synchronizing DoD AI activities to expand Joint Force advantages. A CFT is an inter-disciplinary team including military officers, acquisition officials, and technical experts.

POSITION SUMMARY

  • Location: Arlington, VA | USA
  • Job Type: Permanent
  • Salary: $106,823 - $138,868k
  • Open & Close Dates: 03/29/2022-04/12/2022
  • Number of Openings: 01

The JAIC is looking for Facility Manager to join the Chief of Staff Directorate. In this role, you will handle all the planning, sustainment, and oversight of facility related projects, and executing building space renovations. The ideal candidate has a combination of project management experience, building facilities projects, and interior/renovation tasking’s, while exercising management, operational, and budgetary requirements.

KEY RESPONSIBILITIES

  • Serve as the JAIC facilities technical expert and single point of contact.
  • Evaluate, advise, and coordinate problem resolution to all elements of facility management.
  • Prepare and implement facility standards and procedures for the day-to-day operations of all JAIC facilities.
  • Ensure systems are in proper operating condition, schedule preventive maintenance checks etc.
  • Monitor contracts pertaining to building maintenance.
  • Develop statements of work, and ensure contractor performance aligns to compliance with the statement of work.
  • Coordinate with Building Management Office for maintenance of utilities, custodial service, emergency generator service, grounds maintenance, and custodial services
  • Provide technical building management and facility guidance to JAIC personnel.
  • Conduct periodic visits to all JAIC workspaces to inspect facilities, provide on-site advice and initiate necessary work orders.
  • Manages the JAIC facility work order process to include receiving and approving requirements, scheduling work to be performed, and inspecting/validating all work upon completion.
  • Develops responsive facility designs and implementation plans.
  • Provides technical and administrative direction for facility projects, including multi-year renovations.
  • Prepare justification for long-range projects of facility plans, submit for review and programming approval to JAIC leadership.
  • Coordinate with DISA WSD/JSP, WHS, and GSA.
  • Monitors budgetary status on all facility related funding documents and develops alternative strategies in the event required funding or other resources are not available.
  • Provide comparative cost and benefits.
  • Maintain continuous overview of building systems, custodial services, and building maintenance to determine adequacy and cost effectiveness.
  • Performs other duties as assigned.

KEY QUALIFICATIONS

  • Comprehensive knowledge and understanding of project management techniques, theories and tools long with analytical skills to determine communication, planning, and execution plans for the facility and space management/utilization activities, and to effectively measure program progress.
  • Proficiency in AutoCAD/VISIO and computer aided facilities management tools for managing space; developing scaled plans, specifications, and views using computer aided design and related drafting software.
  • Knowledge of quantitative analysis, long-range planning techniques to develop standards covering space utilization and initial outfitting and transition program requirements.
  • Thorough knowledge of space planning analysis and utilization design concepts, principles, and practices, Furniture Fixtures and Equipment (FF&E) and supporting services design, and integration of other project elements such as minor construction, IT and AV.
  • Comprehensive knowledge of facility elements, appropriate building codes, technical drafting techniques, and Architectural conventions to produce detailed architectural Design Intent Drawings (DIDs), development of Independent Government Cost Estimates (IGCE), and Statement of Works (SOW).
  • Extensive knowledge of effective management principles and practices in conjunction with policies, regulations and procedures on planning and implementation of assigned projects.
  • Extensive knowledge to conduct staffing and utilization surveys to evaluate effectiveness of proposed structure, and develop and present validated staff requirements.
  • In-depth knowledge and understanding of contract responsibilities, applicable laws and practices.
  • Familiarity with the mission objectives, applicable laws, guidance and directives, and various internal and external elements.
  • Strong written and oral communications skills to develop recommendations, justifications, reports, and present briefings to top level management.
