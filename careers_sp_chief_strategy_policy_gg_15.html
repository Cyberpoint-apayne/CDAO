Strategy and Policy Directorate

Chief Of Strategy and Policy, GG 15

INTRODUCTION

The Joint Artificial Intelligence Center (JAIC) was established to enable the Military Departments and Services, Joint Staff, Combatant Commands (CCMDs), Office of the Secretary of Defense (OSD), and other DoD Components, to serve on a Cross-Functional Team (CFT) to swiftly introduce new capabilities and effectively experiment with new operational concepts in support of DoD's warfighting missions and business functions with the overarching goal of accelerating the delivery of AI-enabled capabilities, scaling the Department-wide impact of AI, and synchronizing DoD AI activities to expand Joint Force advantages. A CFT is an inter-disciplinary team including military officers, acquisition officials, and technical experts.

POSITION SUMMARY

The JAIC is looking for a Chief Of Strategy and Policy to lead the lead the S&P Directorate. In this position, you will support the JAIC Director and the DOD Chief Information Officer (CIO) in the establishment of policy, strategic communications and engagement. Specifically, the Division Chief will establish joint training programs, supports interagency and international requirements, builds, and sustains international partnerships. The ideal candidate has the expertise needed to support and guide the development and implementation of tailored and measurable outreach to key stakeholders to ensure JAIC's mission, progress, and relevance is understood by each stakeholder.

KEY RESPONSIBILITIES

  • Lead the development, assessment, and execution of AI training programs to support Defense-wide AI adoption. 
  • Ensure the development of policies, governance documents, technical standards, and manuals meet interagency and Department quality standards.
  • Provide mentorship and ensures collaboration in the areas of training data management, AI safety, security, and assurance, and test and evaluation of algorithms.
  • Oversee the measurement and assessment of pertinent National, Department and Agency strategies to ensure proper alignment of resourcing, operations, and plans is occurring for AI transformation.
  • Develop and coordinate communication with international, national, Congressional, and Department-wide boards and bodies.
  • Oversee the organization representation and messaging to private and public forums and ensures the office and Department of Defense senior leadership are supported in a variety of settings - from speaking events, to testimonies, to media relations.
  • Oversees a team of professionals responsible for the establishment and management of DoD AI governance and policy.
  • Plan directorate resourcing, acquisition strategies, and assign work to be accomplished based on priorities, consideration of the difficulty and requirements of assignments, and employee capabilities.
  • Support and staff JAIC Director, and, as directed, CIO, on external engagements including visits with foreign partners and participation in interagency meetings.
  • Develop and coordinate communication strategies and “talking points” that can be used by the office and Department of Defense senior leadership in a variety of settings (speaking events, to testimonies, to media relations).
  • Manage the coordination of select events that ensure maximum return on investment.
  • Liaison with communications staff from across the Department to ensure messaging alignment and maximum understanding of AI and JAIC as well as the structure of DoD and the current political and strategic landscape of this environment.
  • Performs other duties as assigned.

POSITION REQUIREMENTS

  • Preferred experience in implementing DoD principles and ethics governing AI use for DoD
  • Proven experience in coordinating communication both externally and internally.
  • Ability to inherent in a position at this level and exercise the judgment and ingenuity in guiding personnel in the analysis of unique problems, and the development of new and improved techniques.
  • Ability to exercise responsibility for the planning and execution of all staff functions throughout the JAIC. 
  • Proven experience in coordinating diverse work through intermediate levels of supervision.
  • Proven experience in authority, responsibility, direction for, and execution of an organization wide program budget planning, manpower management, policy formulation, and advisory services. 
  • Experience in technical responsibility for the work of an organization.
  • Experience in overseeing an organization through one or more levels of supervisors.
  • Proven experience in making changes in the organization of work within allowable budget and established policy.
  • Proven experience in established quality standards and internal instructions and procedures. 
  • Proven experience in established operating guidelines to coordinate activities of supervisors in all substantive and management areas.
