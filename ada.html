AI and Data Accelerator (ADA)

Designed to Transform How Combatant Commands Conduct Globally-Integrated Data Management

The AI and Data Accelerator (ADA) is designed to transform how Combatant Commands conduct globally-integrated data management, including both warfighting and business decision analytics, and provide a data foundation to enable workflow and C2 automation capabilities. 

ADA pulls together resources, advanced solutions from across DoD, and cross-functional expertise to build and implement scalable, responsive foundational enterprise capabilities to support AI transformation.  

Ada is:

  • Integrated: Led by JAIC & the DoD Chief Data Officer (CDO), with partners including Joint Staff, DDS, CIO, USD(R&E), USD(I&S), and others. This effort integrates each CCMD with people, platforms, and partnerships from across the JADC2 community.  
  • Innovative: ADA will accelerate work that is already planned and funded. ADA will facilitate the development of global, open architecture data C2 solutions that enable modern data management, advanced analytics, and automated business and warfighting workflows. 
  • Invested:  This effort has the attention of our most senior DoD leaders. DSD Hicks personally announced this effort and the Vice Chairman, JROC, and JADC2 CFT have endorsed the requirement. This work supports implementation of the DoD Data Strategy, JADC2 Strategy, and DSD Data Decrees.
  • Iterative: ADA is a methodology to rapidly identify and scale solutions through a series of implementation sprints and exercises, each one purposely building understanding through successive and incremental learning. This three-year pilot will help identify, prioritize, and sustainably address Joint warfighting challenges and set future requirements.  
  • Impactful: ADA is focused on rapidly delivering decision advantage from the battlespace to the boardroom. 
ADA teams will work closely with each Combatant Command, tailoring data, and building algorithm and infrastructure solutions. ADA will provide embedded elements to enable operators and developers to rapidly iterate and employ agile methodologies ultimately building a foundation of scalable solutions throughout other Combatant Commands and the Department.
