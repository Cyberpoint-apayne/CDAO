Responsible AI (RAI)

Advancements in artificial intelligence (AI) are transforming national security, the global economy, and society at large. Adopting AI will preserve and extend America’s military advantage in a digitally competitive world. As the United States Department of Defense (DoD) embraces AI, it is imperative that this technology is integrated in a manner consistent with our national values, shared democratic ideals, and commitment to lawful and ethical behavior.

The DoD AI Ethical Principles outlines appropriate safeguards for a technology that continues to evolve. The Principles build on and complement the existing ethical, legal, safety, and policy frameworks that are the hallmark of the Department. To take these high level Principles and instantiate them into DoD practices, the Department is adopting Responsible AI (RAI).

Within the DoD, RAI is an approach to the design, development, deployment, and use of AI systems to advance their trustworthiness and ensure their ethical employment. RAI is manifested through testing standards, accountability checks, employment guidance, human systems integration, and safety considerations.

Initial RAI efforts that have been implemented within the DoD’s Joint Artificial Intelligence Center include:

  • Leveraging industry best practices to develop and test tools, such as AI Data Cards and Model Cards, that consider relevant risks from the outset of an AI project and reduce the unintended consequences.
  • Integrating RAI into the Tradewind acquisition business model by piloting AI ethics assessments and checkpoints with vendors.
  • Including contract clauses to demonstrate how a capability addresses the DoD AI Ethical Principles and aids in mitigating ethical risks.
  • Including RAI instruction in the 2020 DoD AI Education Strategy.
  • Promoting RAI internationally with like-minded defense forces through the AI Partnership for Defense [link to International section]

The DoD is taking a holistic, integrated, and disciplined approach to RAI through an RAI Strategy and Implementation Plan. The enterprise-wide efforts will help to scale RAI in a manner that embraces innovation, while adopting a risk management approach to AI development. Through RAI, the DoD provides our Service members, the American public, and our partners and allies justified confidence in DoD’s AI-enabled systems.

"Responsible AI is the place where cutting-edge tech meets timeless values. The Department’s use of AI must reinforce our democratic values, protect our rights, ensure our safety, and defend our privacy,”
- Secretary of Defense, Lloyd Austin


DoD Ethical Principles for AI

  • Responsible: DoD personnel will exercise appropriate levels of judgment and care, while remaining responsible for the development, deployment, and use of AI capabilities.
  • Equitable: The Department will take deliberate steps to minimize unintended bias in AI capabilities.
  • Traceable: The Department’s AI capabilities will be developed and deployed such that relevant personnel possess an appropriate understanding of the technology, development processes, and operational methods applicable to AI capabilities, including with transparent and auditable methodologies, data sources, and design procedure and documentation.
  • Reliable: The Department’s AI capabilities will have explicit, well-defined uses, and the safety, security, and effectiveness of such capabilities will be subject to testing and assurance within those defined uses across their entire life cycles.
  • Governable: The Department will design and engineer AI capabilities to fulfill their intended functions while possessing the ability to detect and avoid unintended consequences, and the ability to disengage or deactivate deployed systems that demonstrate unintended behavior.
