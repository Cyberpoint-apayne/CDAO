AI ADOPTION JOURNEY

Introduction

The Department of Defense is at a transformational moment in history. AI is rewriting almost every aspect of business and society. Data, predictive analytics and AI are powering financial systems, production industries, business-decision-making, retail markets, social media, and services delivery. The DoD must do the same thing. We must adopt AI to transform how we operate and fight as a Joint Force.

This is not easy. Soldiers, Sailors, Airmen, Marines and Guardians must think differently about their challenges, and find proven, data-enabled and machine-teaming solutions. We wants to help those who are thinking about using AI along their “AI Adoption Journey.” The suite of videos, documents, and other information is yours to explore, and will help you map out the critical milestones along your journey to modern and relevant AI-enabled capabilities.

AI Adoption Journey Map

Needs Assessment and Readiness
The first step of the journey is often the hardest - identifying the needs and appropriately managing the resources for the project. Hear from the team that can help you navigate the path from problem identification (and if AI is a viable option), to production and deployment of your desired solution. We do this by matching expertise and resources to meet your specific needs. For more information on how to map out your journey, click here.

AI Training and Education
To successfully adopt AI, you need to understand it – or at least understand the parts that are critical for your role. The Training and Education team is delivering training programs to provide every level of the DoD Workforce with knowledge that will help them be AI-Ready. Whether you are Leading, Driving, Creating, Embedding, Facilitating, or Employing AI, there are training opportunities for you. Click here to find out what training is available.

Concept Development
So you want to “do AI.” What does that mean, really? During the concept development part of the journey, we will help move your project from a theoretical understanding to practical application. From understanding your current data and AI-related technology infrastructure to outlining what a potential minimum viable product (MVP) might look like, we will help you get where you want to go. For more information on Concept Development, click here.

Technology Scouting
Just like looking for recommendations on places to go and things to see on a trip, tech scouting is important because it enables you to save money as you identify proven technologies to meet your needs. By leveraging our lessons learned, knowledge, contacts, and understanding of the AI ecosystem, we will help you stay on top of industry trends and current products to solve your specific challenge. Click here to learn more about finding the right solution.

Funding
How do you plan for a future AI program when the technology is changing so rapidly and yet funding is often a regimented process? This step in your AI adoption Journey focuses on the expertise available to help organizations forecast AI into the future, execute a budget in the current year, and identify an AI acquisition solution. Click here to learn how to be agile as you fiscally prepare for an AI project.

Acquisition
Rapid Done Right… Acquisition enables every single step of the AI adoption journey - from needs assessment, to technology scouting, to funding, to competition and award, and finally to delivery and performance measuring. Click here to learn about the tools and vehicles available to enable rapid access to emerging AI technologies with the diligence of market knowledge and acquisition expertise.

Capability Development
This step of the AI adoption journey focuses on finding the infrastructure that’s right for your project. Bringing new AI capabilities to the DoD can be challenging and you must have essential DevSecOps infrastructure in place to collect, store and transform raw data to be AI-Ready; to write code to develop then train models; test for adherence to responsible AI; then deliver for operational use by the Warfighter into data that is AI-ready. Sounds hard right? Well, the Joint Common Foundation provides many of these tools within a cloud infrastructure designed to adhere to Zero Trust Architecture principles. Click here to learn how the JCF will develop an AI platform and manage the infrastructure, so you can focus on what’s most important – AI experimentation and development.

AI Implementation and Sustainment
While this may be the last stage of the AI adoption journey, it’s now time to put the solution to work. AI implementation and sustainment is a continuous process with software updates, AI model retraining, and occasional hardware upgrades. It is also time to think about training people to use the system and revamping workflows for maximum efficiency. It’s a continuous lifecycle of data, training, and upgrades. Watch this video to learn how to ensure long-term success of your program.
